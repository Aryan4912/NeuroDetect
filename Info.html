Understanding Brain Tumors

Brain tumors are abnormal growths of cells in the brain or surrounding tissues. Some are benign (non-cancerous), while others are malignant (cancerous), and early detection is crucial for effective treatment and better outcomes.

Types of Brain Tumors

  • Gliomas: Tumors originating from glial cells, common and often aggressive.
  • Meningiomas: Develop in the membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord, usually benign.
  • Pituitary Tumors: Found in the pituitary gland, can affect hormone levels.
  • Medulloblastomas: Affects children more, starts in the cerebellum.
  • Schwannomas: Affect nerve sheaths, often benign.

Symptoms

  • Persistent headaches
  • Seizures
  • Vision or hearing problems
  • Memory loss or confusion
  • Difficulty with balance or coordination
  • Nausea or vomiting, especially in the morning

Importance of Early Detection

Detecting tumors early can significantly increase the chances of successful treatment. It helps in reducing the complexity of surgeries, the extent of medication, and improves survival rates. Our goal is to make early diagnosis accessible even in remote areas lacking specialized medical professionals.
